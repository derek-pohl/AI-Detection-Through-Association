The "Association" Method

When a topic is given, both humans and AI draw from a well of associated concepts. However, the "shape" of this well is different for each. AI, trained on vast datasets, tends to retrieve the most common and statistically likely associations. Humans, with their unique experiences and memories, often make more varied or unexpected connections.

Example: When asked about the Industrial Revolution, AI might consistently mention steam engines, factory conditions, and urbanization. A human might focus on specific regional impacts, personal family stories, or lesser-known historical details. If a submitted text shares more ideas that often pop up in AI generated text, it too may be AI generated.

By comparing the core ideas of a submitted text to a freshly-generated AI text on the same topic, we can measure the conceptual overlap. A high degree of overlap suggests that the text likely originated from a similar "thought" process as the AI, indicating a higher probability of it being AI-generated.

Guide to Using the Detector

To get the most accurate results from this detector, consider the following:

  • Submit full paragraphs or essays: The detector works best with complete thoughts and developed ideas, not just short sentences or bullet points.
  • Use analytical or descriptive text: The "association" method is most effective when analyzing texts that explore concepts, provide explanations, or describe scenarios.
  • Understand the probabilistic nature: This tool provides a likelihood assessment, not a definitive "yes" or "no." AI detection is an evolving field, and results should be interpreted as strong indicators rather than absolute proof.
